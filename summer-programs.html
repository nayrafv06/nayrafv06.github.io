Summer Programs

San Ysidro Teen Clinic

Fall '21 - Fall '23

Being a part of the San Ysidro Teen Clinic as a Peer Health Advocate (PHA) we educate our community by doing community outreach, we host an open house once a year to inform community members of our services and give some sexual health information

During my internship I learned about sexual health, mental health, and health disparities within my community. I was able to get a certification in QPR, I learned about the different ways that I can help my community and I was able to teach other people about what they can do to stay safe.

During my second year on this internship I became a mentor and helped other PHAs understand topics while also helping organize community events. I led ice breakers and shared my experience with other people so that they would also be interested in becoming a PHA.

California State Summer School for Mathematics and Science - COSMOS

Summer '23

In this program we created an autonomous vehicle using donkey sim for behavior cloning. We also trained the car with gnss coordinates and used a vesc to set up the direction of the motors and the way the car would move.

During the duration of the program I learned about embedded linux, coding with python, how to set up electronics, human behavior cloning, and programming PID controllers for our robots to drive smoother paths.

I contributed to this project by using my CAD skills to create the base plate where all of the electronics would go, I created a model using a donkey sim with a combination of my driving as well as my teammates. Then I worked on sshing into our jetson nano to start training our car with our model. I was able to drive the car around and record its path in order to use waypoints which I then fixed with PID controllers.

UCSD Upward Bound Summer Residential Program

Summer '22, Summer '23

During the Upward Bound Summer Residential Program I took classes on math, english, science, language, and a senior seminar. I was able to take an introductory class for calculus, a chemistry course, I learned the basics of Arabic, and for my last year doing this program I took an introductory class to statistics.

During my first year in the program I learned about integrals, the basics of a new foreign language, and forensic chemistry where we study our fingerprints. My last year doing this program I was able to get an introduction on statistics, and I learned about the different college systems while getting time to work on my personal statement and a head start on my PIQs for the UC applications.

First Gen Scholars

Summer '23

During my time on the summer sweetwater cohort we were able to get information on the school systems, and one-on-one help from mentors with our college applications. I was able to work on my personal statement and they taught me how to build a college list and choose the different colleges that I should apply to based on help offered for me as well as resources available to first generation students.
During the duration of the summer portion of this program I learned how to cope with stress and anxiety during the college application season, I also learned how to cope with imposter syndrome. I was given information on the different ways that I could approach college applications and I was able to spend most of my time brainstorming and working on my personal statement.
